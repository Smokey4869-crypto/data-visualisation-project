Stacked Bar Chart

The stacked bar chart is a comprehensive visualization that presents the reasons for people's movement from 2010 to 2019 in the United States. The chart displays the four main categories of reasons: family-related, housing-related, employment-related, and others. Each bar represents a specific mobility period, and the height of the bar indicates the percentage distribution of reasons.

Stacked Pie Chart

The stacked pie chart provides an in-depth analysis of the reasons behind people's movement in the United States. This chart consists of two layers: an inner layer representing the four main reasons (family-related, housing-related, employment-related, and others) and an outer layer showcasing the corresponding sub-reasons. Each sub-reason is represented by a segment, with the color intensity indicating its percentage contribution within the main reason category.
