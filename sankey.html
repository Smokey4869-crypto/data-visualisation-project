About

The Sankey diagram depicts the migration patterns between continents and different legal immigration statuses. The diagram showcases the migration flows from various continents to different immigration statuses, providing insights into the global movement of people. Each continent is represented as a source node, and the immigration statuses are shown as destination nodes. The width of the flow lines corresponds to the quantity of migrants, offering a clear visual representation of the relative magnitudes of immigration across continents and immigration statuses

The source of data is collected from United States Department of Homeland Security
